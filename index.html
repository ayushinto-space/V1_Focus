V1 Focus converts your boring hours into structured aviator flights, leveraging acoustics and psychology to build focus.

🎯

The Psychology of V1

In aviation, V1 is the critical speed beyond which the aircraft must continue its takeoff regardless of anomalies. By initiating a focus flight, you enter a mental commitment of sitting through the journey just like a real flight.

🎧

Auditory Masking

Distraction is fundamentally triggered by sudden volumes (e.g. footsteps, slamming doors). Generating a continuous sound raises your environment's "noise floor." Ensuring sudden sounds are completely masked before reaching the auditory cortex.

🗺️

Spatial Progress

Your work duration is tied to real distance using live transponder data. Your mind naturally associates air travel with isolation and active "Airplane Mode," this creates an environment of uninterrupted workflow.

📸

Verified Boarding Pass

Upon initialization the system constructs a digital boarding pass of your sesssion. Export it instantly to let your friends and folks know that you have embarked on a silent journey.

FLIGHT CREW MANUAL

Standard Pre-Flight Checklist

01 / VECTOR SELECTION

File Your Manifest

Navigate to Flight Control and enter a flight number (e.g., EK342, AA210). You can also drop in quick presets like HOP or CRUISE.

💡 TIP: Look up any currently active commercial flight on Flightradar24 to copy a real-world flight, sitting at your desk.
02 / RADAR LOCK

Initialize Track

Click the Track button. The system validates your path vectors, maps your spatial progress trajectory across coordinates and builds your custom digital Boarding Pass.

03 / DEPARTURE RUN

Cross V1 Commit

Hit Clear for Departure to lock the deck. Adjust your Engine Noise slider to your exact isolation preference to lift the room's noise floor and shield your attention.

04 / CRUISE STABILITY

Maintain Clean Air

Keep your tab active. Navigating away, minimizing windows, or breaking focus registers as ambient turbulence. Touch down safely to record completed sectors into your permanent Pilot Logbook.

Ready for departure?

Head to the control desk to map your transponder code or load a isolation sandbox environment.
